Programs Offered by The Innovators Schooling System

Kidspreurship & Agripreneurship Program
Creating Entrepreneurs from the Classroom

At The Innovators Schooling System, Kidspreurship and Agripreneurship are not extracurricular activitiesâ€”they are core strategic programs integrated into the Primary and Lower Secondary Sections to cultivate an entrepreneurial mindset from an early age. This forward-thinking curriculum is research-designed and future-oriented, with a clear objective: to develop entrepreneurs at school level and enable students to initiate real startups during their academic journey.

Program Philosophy

The Kidspreurship & Agripreneurship Program is built on the belief that entrepreneurship is not inheritedâ€”it is developed through structured learning, practice, and mentorship. Students are systematically nurtured to think like innovators, problem-solvers, and value creators rather than job seekers.

Program Structure & Approach

  • Introduced from Primary Grades and strengthened through Lower Secondary
  • Progressive, age-appropriate learning pathways
  • Hands-on, project-based, and experiential methodology
  • Real-world business simulations and startup models
  • Mentorship-driven learning environment

Core Learning Domains

  • Entrepreneurial Mindset Development: Opportunity identification, problem-solving, creativity, innovation, resilience, leadership, risk assessment, and decision-making.
  • Business Understanding: Fundamentals of business models, value creation, understanding products, services, customers, markets, supply chains, and operations.
  • Financial Literacy: Money management, budgeting, costing, pricing, profit & loss, savings & reinvestment, ethical financial behavior, and responsibility.
  • Market Research & Consumer Insight: Identifying customer needs and market gaps, basic research techniques, data interpretation, competitive analysis, and product positioning.
  • Selling & Communication Skills: Sales techniques, persuasion, customer engagement, presentation, negotiation, pitching skills, brand communication, and storytelling.
  • Decision-Making & Strategic Thinking: Analytical decision-making, evaluating risks and outcomes, accountability, and leadership judgment.
  • Agripreneurship Focus (Sustainability & Green Economy): Agriculture-based enterprises, sustainable farming, agri-innovation, green technologies, environmental responsibility, social entrepreneurship, and practical sustainability projects.

Real Impact & Outcomes

As students progress into the Secondary Section, they are capable of initiating real startups, earning through school-supported entrepreneurial ventures, applying business concepts in real market environments, and developing independence, confidence, and financial awareness.

Graduate Profile (Program Outcomes)

  • Entrepreneurially driven innovators
  • Financially literate and market-aware individuals
  • Confident decision-makers and problem-solvers
  • Ethical business thinkers with social responsibility
  • Future-ready leaders prepared for global economic challenges
